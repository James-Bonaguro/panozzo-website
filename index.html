Our Story

A Century of Serving Families in the Southland

In 1926, Dionisio “Dan” Panozzo and his brother Louis opened a funeral home in Chicago’s Roseland neighborhood on 115th Street. Immigrants from Treschè Conca in the Veneto region of Italy, the Panozzo brothers built their reputation serving the families of St. Anthony of Padua parish and the broader Italian-American community. They lived above the funeral home, and the business became a pillar of the neighborhood—donating parish calendars every New Year’s Day and quietly supporting parish events for decades.

The family eventually moved the business to its current home at 530 W. 14th Street in Chicago Heights. The second generation—Lawrence “Larry” Panozzo, who served 67 years as a funeral director and was known for greeting every family from his stool at the front door, and Dennis P. Panozzo, who dedicated 63 years to the profession and was deeply involved in Italian-American civic organizations—carried the tradition forward with quiet devotion.

Today, Allen Panozzo leads the firm as the third-generation director. A former senior auditor at Deloitte, Allen brings both professional rigor and the personal warmth his family has been known for across a century of service. Even after leaving Roseland, former residents of Roseland, Pullman, and Kensington frequently choose to come back to Panozzo Bros. for funerals—a testament to the trust this family has built over 100 years.
