How to Write Your Anniversary Message

The homepage has copy that captures the facts about your family's 100 years, but the anniversary really deserves something in your own voice. I know you mentioned you keep trying to get around to writing it. Here is a low-pressure way to get there:

  1. Go for a walk. No phone, no notes. Just let your mind wander about what 100 years means—what your grandfather started, what Larry and Dennis built, and what you want the next chapter to look like.
  2. When you get back, record yourself. Open your phone's voice recorder and talk for 5–10 minutes. Don't try to be polished. Just talk like you're telling a friend.
  3. Transcribe it. Use your phone's built-in speech-to-text, or a free tool like Otter.ai. You'll have a rough transcript in minutes.
  4. Pull out the 3–4 sentences that feel most honest. Read it back and highlight the parts that sound like you. That's the real stuff.
  5. That becomes your anniversary message. We can drop it into the hero section or about section of the homepage. It doesn't have to be long—3–5 sentences from the heart is more powerful than a full page of polished copy.

Quick version: Walk → Record yourself talking → Transcribe → Pull out the best 3–4 lines → Done.

What This Prototype Is (and Isn't)

  • This is a working static homepage you can open in any browser—desktop or mobile

  • This is not connected to your current website or hosting—it's a standalone prototype
  • The obituary links point to panozzobros.com/obituaries—verify this is the correct path on your live site
  • No backend, no forms, no database—just HTML and CSS that can be deployed anywhere

Technical Details

  • No dependencies: No frameworks, no build tools, no JavaScript libraries—just clean HTML and CSS
