# Panozzo Bros. Funeral Home — 100th Anniversary Homepage Prototype

Allen,

This is a working prototype of what a modernized Panozzo Bros. homepage could look like. It is not connected to your live website — it is a standalone page you can open in any browser (on your phone, tablet, or computer) to see the direction.

To preview it, just open the file called "index.html" in your browser. Everything is self-contained.


## Why Now

Your current website has served you well, but this is the year to update it. Here is what I noticed when I audited the site:

- **It does not display properly on phones.** Most people searching for a funeral home are doing it from their phone, often in a moment of need. Right now, the site is difficult to read and navigate on mobile.
- **The 100th anniversary is not featured anywhere.** This is a once-in-a-lifetime milestone. Families should see "100 years" the moment they land on your site — it immediately communicates trust and longevity.
- **Your Google reviews are not on the site.** People check reviews before calling. If they have to leave your website to find them on Google, you are making it harder for them to choose you.
- **The legacy story is not being told.** Three generations — Dan, Larry and Dennis, and now you — that is an incredible story. It should be front and center, not buried or missing.
- **The service area is too narrow.** The site says Chicago Heights, but you serve families across Orland Park, Tinley Park, Mokena, Homer Glen, Frankfort, and beyond. Those communities should see themselves represented.

None of this is a criticism of what you have built over the years. It is just that the website has not kept pace with what the business actually is — and this anniversary is the perfect reason to fix that.


## What Is Different in This Prototype (and Why)

Here is what this version does differently, and the thinking behind each change:

**100th Anniversary as the Centerpiece** — The hero section leads with "Celebrating 100 Years of Family Service" and a large 1926–2026 badge. This is the single most powerful trust signal you have. It should be the first thing people see.

**Your Real Heritage Story** — The about section tells the full three-generation story: Dan and Louis opening on 115th Street in Roseland, Larry greeting families from his stool at the door for 67 years, Dennis dedicating 63 years to the profession, and you carrying it forward today. This is not generic funeral home copy — it is your family's story, and it matters.

**Google Reviews Section** — There is a new section with placeholder review cards. Once you drop in real reviews from your Google listing, this becomes instant social proof. Families see what other families have said without ever leaving your site.

**Expanded Service Areas** — The homepage now lists Chicago Heights, Orland Park, Tinley Park, Mokena, Homer Glen, Frankfort, Orland Hills, Palos Hills, and Lansing — plus a nod to the Roseland, Pullman, and Kensington roots where it all started. This helps families in those communities feel like you are their local funeral home, because you are.

**Mobile-Friendly Design** — The entire site works on phones, tablets, and desktops. The layout adjusts automatically. Text is large and readable. Buttons are easy to tap. The phone number is always visible at the top.

**Clearer Navigation** — There are clear paths for the most common needs: immediate help, services overview, pre-planning, obituaries, and your story. A family in crisis can find the phone number and next steps in seconds.

**Facebook Link** — Your Facebook page is now linked in the footer so families can find you there too.


## What You Need to Do

Here are the action items to make this prototype ready for a real launch:

1. **Replace the placeholder Google reviews.** The reviews section has three cards with bracketed placeholder text. Go to your Google Business listing, pick your three best reviews, and swap them in. (Detailed instructions are in the companion notes file.)

2. **Add photos.** The prototype has no images right now — it is all typography and layout. A photo of the funeral home exterior, a historic photo from the early days if you have one, and a portrait of you would make a big difference.

3. **Write your anniversary message.** I know you have been trying to get around to this. I included a tip in the companion notes file — the short version is: go for a walk, come back and record yourself talking about what 100 years means to you for five minutes, transcribe it, and pull out the best three or four sentences. That becomes your message. It does not have to be polished — it has to be honest. If you want help shaping it after, send me the transcript.

4. **Verify the Facebook link.** The footer links to facebook.com/panozzobros — make sure that is the correct URL for your page.

5. **Verify the obituary link.** The obituary buttons link to panozzobros.com/obituaries — confirm that is the right path on your current site.

6. **Decide if you want to move forward.** This is a prototype to show you what is possible. If you like the direction, the next step is turning this into your live site.
